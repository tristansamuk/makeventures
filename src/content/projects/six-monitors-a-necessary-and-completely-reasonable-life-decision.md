## The Problem With Five Monitors

You probably think the problem with five monitors is that it's too many. That's because you're thinking about it wrong. The problem with five monitors is that it's an odd number, and asymmetry is cognitively taxing. I read that somewhere. The point is, once you're at five, six is basically a health decision.

My previous setup was three monitors, and frankly I don't know how I survived. I'd be writing an email and I couldn't *also* see my calendar, my terminal, my second terminal, my music, and my doorbell camera. I was constantly *moving windows around*. Like an animal.

## The Current Layout

- **Monitor 1 (Center-Left):** Primary work. Documents, IDE, browser. The nerve center.
- **Monitor 2 (Center-Right):** Secondary browser. This is an important distinction from the primary browser. Different use case entirely.
- **Monitor 3 (Far Left):** Email and Slack. I keep this in my peripheral vision so I can see messages arriving without technically "checking" them, which protects my focus.
- **Monitor 4 (Far Right):** Terminal windows. Sometimes a spreadsheet. A man needs a place for his spreadsheets.
- **Monitor 5 (Top Center):** System monitoring dashboard — CPU, RAM, network traffic, and a world clock set to four time zones despite the fact that I work exclusively with people in my same time zone.
- **Monitor 6 (Top Right):** Rotated vertically. I've used it for reading long articles (twice), a Twitch stream (frequently), and currently it's displaying a slideshow of NASA deep space imagery, which I feel sets a tone.

## The Mount Situation

Six monitors don't just *go* somewhere. The mount is rated for four monitors. I'm running six off it by supplementing with a secondary arm bolted to a different part of the desk, and a third arm clamped to a shelf unit I moved next to the desk specifically for this purpose. The shelf unit is no longer being used as a shelf. It is now load-bearing monitor infrastructure.

My desk is 84 inches wide. With all six monitors deployed I have approximately 11 inches of usable surface remaining. I keep a coffee mug there. The mug has to be placed precisely, like I'm playing Operation, because bumping the shelf unit causes Monitor 6 to tilt two degrees, and then I have to re-level it with a phone app I downloaded for this specific problem.

## The GPU Question

My current card supports four monitors. I've resolved this by also installing an old GPU from 2019 that I had in a box. The two GPUs coexist peacefully inside my PC, which runs at a temperature I'm choosing not to investigate.

About twice a week I'll sit down and Monitor 4 will just be black. The fix is to unplug it and plug it back in. I've done this so many times that the DisplayPort connector has developed what I can only describe as a personality — it needs to be inserted at a slight upward angle, held for two seconds, and then released slowly. My wife has seen me do this and did not ask questions. We are nine years into this marriage and she has calibrated her curiosity accordingly.

## Productivity Impact

Before six monitors, I was operating at around 60–65% of my cognitive potential. With six monitors, that number is much higher. I don't have an exact figure because I haven't set up the productivity tracking dashboard yet.

What I can tell you is that I *feel* more productive. I feel, when I sit down in the morning and all six screens flicker to life, like a person who is in control of things. My actual work output is about the same as before, but I attribute this to external factors.

## What's Next

I've been looking at ultrawides. Not to replace anything — to supplement. That would bring the total to seven monitors, which I recognize sounds excessive.

But seven is an odd number, so realistically we're talking about eight.
